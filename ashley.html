Zines Had it Right All Along

"Zines have an enormous variety. They’re experimental and diverse. This gives them a freshness and surprise. They’re anti-formalist. They’re relatable”

Kawandeep Virdee

Digital will reflect more qualities that make print great.

Specifically quarterly magazines and zines. Quarterlies are a pleasure to read, with variety in layout and pacing. They're beautiful to hold.

They're less frequent, and much better. Even the ads are well crafted, and trusted.

Example:

Posture + Anxy

Zines have an...

Enormous variety

Experimental and diverse

Relateable

Freshness and surprise

Anti-formalist

Are these qualities possible in digital? (Why don't we see more of them) Digital is expressive and relateable. But most sites look the same.

IT CAN BE WEIRD AND WONDERFUL.

See: bloopdance.com

Here's a recent example. Internetting with Amanda Hess by the New York Times. Art Direction is 100 100

The variety of layout and pacing keeps it interesting to consume. It is experimental. It is both serious + playful. It is deep and it is poppy. They are incredibly well made and fresh. The best of quarterlies and zines.

News media will adopt these qualities. It will be a strategic advantage.
